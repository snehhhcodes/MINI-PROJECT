Description

The Bankey Bihari Temple, a mysterious dwelling of Lord Krishna, is located in the holy town of Vrindavan, which is perched on the banks of the Yamuna River.


Enchanting Stories

  • In a tale of divine manifestation, saint Swami Haridas ,immersed in profound meditation, yearned for a tangible form of Lord Krishna. In response to his deep devotion, Krishna himself appeared as a self-manifested idol, now enshrined as the primary deity in the Bankey Bihari Temple.
  • Named Bankey Bihari for his captivating presence, the idol of Lord Krishna in this temple is believed by devotees to exude a unique charm. To preserve the intimacy between the deity and his devotees, a curtain veils the idol's eyes. This curtain is briefly unveiled, granting devotees a glimpse of the enchanting form of Bankey Bihari.
