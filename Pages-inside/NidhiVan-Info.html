Nidhivan A Sacred Woodland Steeped In Mythic Tales

  1. Home
  2. Nidhivan

Description

Nidhivan is renowned for its enchanting aura, a place where spirituality intertwines with the whispers of ancient trees. The air is thick with a sense of reverence, attracting seekers and believers alike. This lush and sacred woodland holds tales that dance on the edge of reality and the ethereal, inviting visitors into a realm where the divine meets the mysterious.


Enchanting Stories

  • According to local folklore, Lord Krishna, along with his beloved Radha and the gopis, engaged in nightly Ras Leela (divine dance) at Nidhivan. The grove is believed to witness their eternal play even today. Some believe that the trees change forms during the night, as if participating in the divine play.
  • Nidhivan is an integral part of the Brij Yatra, a pilgrimage that takes devotees to various places associated with Lord Krishna. A particular tree in Nidhivan, known as the Ropani Tree is considered extremely sacred. Legend has it that Radha and Krishna performed their Ras Leela beneath its branches, and devotees often pay homage to this divine site.
